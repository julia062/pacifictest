August 2, 1942

Great Lakes, IL

Dear Sis,

This has sure been a long day. We had to get up at 5:30 this morning. We had chow at 6:30, then cleaned up the barracks and there wasn’t anything to do all day.

I sat around the barracks all morning and this afternoon we walked all over the camp.

The fellows certainly are friendly around here. They call everybody “MAC!” When you walk down the street all you here is, “Where are you from Mac?” or “Hey ya Mac, got a dime for two nickels?” There are machines around here that you put a dime in and you get a cup of chocolate milk.

Tomorrow I think we’ll get our uniforms and I suppose a haircut. I wish you could see some of the haircuts around here.There are about seven barbers and they race to see who can cut the fastest. I guess it takes about three minutes to a head. One guy was telling me that when he got his, the barber asked him if he wanted his sideburns. He said “yes.” The barber said, “Okay, catch them,” and he ran the clipper right over his head.

We sure got good chow today. For dinner we had chicken and dumplings, mashed potatoes, vegetables, tomato soup, a salad, bread and butter, ice cream, and lemonade.

I might send my clothes home one of these first days. I can keep my wrist watch, but they don’t allow pocket watches.

I’m going to buy a money belt tomorrow. It isn’t a very safe place to have much money. I spent about $5.00 coming up on the train, but that’s about all I’ve spent.

I tried to get in the canteen today, but there was such a long line there, I gave up. There are longer ones in the other camps I guess. I think I’ll be moved soon. My address will still be the same though.

I think Harry is in another camp because I walked all over this camp today and I didn’t see him.

Love,

Donnie
