August 25, 1942

Great Lakes, IL

Dear Sis,

Thanks a lot for the cake. It was swell. The frosting was crumbled a little but not bad. The envelopes come in pretty handy too. It’s almost impossible to buy them down here.

I’ve been kind of slow in writing but I’ve really been busy this week. 85 of our men are on K.P. and the rest of us have to stand barracks watches and fire guard. The watches are 4 hrs. Long and I get about 2 or 3 of them each day. I was on this afternoon but I'll get to sleep tonight, I guess.

I was on a “work detail” yesterday and I finally got out of this camp for a change. We went to Camp Lawrence. It’s about 3 ½ miles away. I didn’t realize this place was so big. There are 7 camps here, I guess. I’m out of detention now and I plan to go over and see Harry as soon as I can get a leave. I have just 12 days left now and I’m counting the days until I get out of here. I plan to take the 4:00 home. About the closest I can get to home is Waseca. I’ll get in there about 10:30 in the evening. I have been chumming with a couple of fellows from there and they told me they would take me home from there. I get my first liberty next Saturday. I haven’t decided just where I’ll go yet. They say Milwaukee is a pretty good place to go. I broke the crystal on my watch and I haven’t been wearing it for about a week now. I think I will be able to get it fixed on my liberty. Well, that's about all I can think of right now and I guess it’s about chow time anyways.

Love,

Donnie
