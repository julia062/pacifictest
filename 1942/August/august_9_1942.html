August 9, 1942

Great Lakes, IL

Dear Sis,

Well, it’s Sunday and there isn’t much to do. I was on guard duty this morning from 7 to 12, so I didn’t go to church. They compel everyone to go here. There were a couple of fellows in our company that had never been to church.

There was a sort of a storm here last night and it rained almost all morning. There was a regular flood in Chicago Friday night I guess.

I haven’t had much for excitement since I got here. They have shows three times a week, but I haven’t gone to any yet. Yesterday, there was a baseball game. D. L. played Cleveland. Half the company went. I was one of the unlucky ones. Great Lakes plays the St. Louis Cardinals tomorrow and I may go to that or the “Happy Hour” radio program tomorrow night.

Sure glad to hear your grain was so good and you must be plenty busy now that you’re doing the chores and everything. I suppose Ellen Louise gets wilder every day. I sort of miss her.

Carmen told me that Magnel Rame was engaged. I almost fell over when I read that. I sure didn't know anything about it.

I didn’t get time to go down to see Nels when I was in Minneapolis but I plan to stop there when I come home on my nine day leave. I’m sure looking forward to that. So far as I know now I’ll graduate the 7th of Sept. I hope to get home before Melvin goes to the army.

I went down to see about having my picture taken last night. I can get a half dozen plain pictures in nice folders and one tinted one in a frame with the navy insignia on it for about $6.50. I think I’ll wait about 3 weeks though. My hair will be a little longer by that time (I hope). By the way, have you had that film developed that you took in Mankato. I didn’t see you there when we got on the bus.

I think I’ll take that $5.00 that I got from Uncle John and buy a new watch band on my first liberty. I bent the buckle on mine a little. Time sure goes slow here. I count the days until I get that nine day leave. I’ll have to celebrate my birthday on the way home I guess. There surely isn’t any way of celebrating it here. I think I’ll go down to the canteen now and get some ink.

Love,

Donnie
