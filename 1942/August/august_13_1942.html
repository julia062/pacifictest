August 13, 1942

Great Lakes, IL

Dear Sis,

I got your letter today and also one from Ruth. I got the Register too. Tell Arnold, “thanks a lot.” It sure was swell of him. Maybe, I’ll get time to write to him one of these days. I got 3 letters and two cards yesterday, so I’ve got a lot to write. I got letters from Nels, Aunt Dina, and Betty, and a card from May and Mrs. Hamson. I answered some of them last night. I guess Dina had a swell vacation.

I’ve had a swell day today. This morning we went down to the lake and went out in the boat. We went way out in the lake and it sure was fun. It’s the first time I’ve seen the lake. There sure was some big waves out there. This afternoon, they marched us about 20 miles and then we went swimming. I’ll get a lot. We have to be able to swim 50 yards and I can swim only about 10.

I washed all my clothes before clow this afternoon and then went to the canteen afterwards. It’s 6:30 now and I haven't anything to do but just write. You’ll notice I finally remembered to get ink. I don’t know if I told you that we all put a quarter a piece in on a radio for the barracks. They’re playing “Deep in the Heart of Texas” now and all the fellows are singing with it. They sure can make a lot of noise. By the way did you ever hear “The Ambassadors” play on the radio. It is a symphony orchestra. They played all over in Minnesota in different colleges. Mr. King the leader is in our company. He sings in the Navy choir. He is a wonderful singer.

I’ll get some more shots tomorrow for typhoid fever. I don’t like to think about it.

Sure glad to hear Clarence got so much for his hogs. I’ll bet you like your new stove. I’m sure glad you could get it now.

Betty told me Clarence Thompson and Jenny were married. I knew Donnie Zielie was going to get married. I’ll bet Melvin and Ellis had a good time celebrating Ellis’s Birthday. I would have liked to have been along.

Betty said she seen them pull out of Podunk that night. She was wondering where they were going. I kind of miss Ellis. I suppose he is just as wild as ever.

So Harold is still going with Mary Ethel. He’s really got something there and I’m not kidding. I think I’ll write to her and kid her along a little. By the way, how is Aunt Anna feeling now. I’ll have to write her.

One fellow here got 3 letters from the same girl today. She really must think he is alright.

One of my pals (or should I say mate?) got a big box of candy and stuff yesterday so I’ve been eating plenty good. You asked me what I wanted instead of the camera. Just send me some of your cooking. This chow they feed us up here is terrible and some home baking really tastes swell. That’s a pretty good hint isn’t it? Ha! Don’t bother if you’re busy though with chores and everything. I’d sure like to see Ellen Louise now. I suppose she gets cuter every day.

I found out today that we get out of detention on the 24th. Our first liberty will be about the 27th and I’ll graduate either the 4th or 7th of Sept. so it won’t be long before I’ll be some again.

I haven’t decided where I’ll go on my liberty yet but it will either be Racine, Wis. or Chicago. It’s about the same distance.

That's about all I can think of now and I’ll have to write to Ruth tonight so,

Lots of Love,

Donnie

P.S. I just read this over and I don’t see how anybody can understand it. I haven’t got time to write it over. It would be just as bad anyway.
