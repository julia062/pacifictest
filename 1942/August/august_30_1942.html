August 30, 1942

Great Lakes, IL

Dear Sis,

I’m going to try and write you a few lines, but I don’t know how it will go. The tables are all filled up and I’m laying on the floor.

I don’t know what has happened to everybody down there. I’ve only received 3 letters all week. For a while I was getting 5 a day. Well, anyways I don’t have so many to answer.

Well, I had my first liberty yesterday. I went to Kenosha, Wisconsin. It’s about 20 miles north of here. It’s an awfully pretty town. It’s right on the edge of the lake and there is a sort of a harbor that runs right up into the town. It’s quite a large town about 50,000 people. We got in there about 4:30 in the afternoon and I had my watch fixed first. Then we bowled a couple times and then went down to the U.S.A. club and had supper. That sure is a swell place. They have places to read, play cards, eat, write letters, listen to the radio or anything you may wish to do. They have large bowls of cigarettes and they told us we could take all we wanted to. They had sandwiches, pie, cake, cookies, fruit, milk, or coffee and they told us to eat all we could.

We got in about 11:15 and it sure was fun watching some of the others come in. There were a few in our company that got pretty drunk and they had quite a time sleeping in a hammock. The guy that sleeps next to unlatched his and laid it on the floor. He fell out 3 times before he did that though. I planned on calling Harry Friday night and meet him somewhere Saturday but I didn’t get time.

I don’t know whether you can understand this or not, but I’ll try another page.

I am sending you my insurance policy. You can put it in a safe place and be sure not to lose it because it’s the only one you will get.

I may send you my company picture too. I’ll have to go down and get some stamps first.

The next time you go to Blue Earth, you can go into the newspaper office and change my address to Bricelyn. So far as I know now I’ll be home the 7th or 8th of September.

We are going out on the the rifle range tomorrow for rifle practice and Tuesday. I’ll find out where I made service school or not. My grades were all pretty high except math and that's pretty important, I guess. I signed up for Diesel as my first choice and Gunners’ mate as my second. I doubt very much whether I made it although I made 87 in my mechanical aptitude test. If I’d had a little better average in math I would have a better chance.

Well, I’ll have to stop for a while. I’m a sort of a big shot today. Our corporal of the Guards is out on liberty and I’m taking over his duties. I’ll have to round up all the guards that go on duty this afternoon and march them to early chow and then see too that they get on their post in time.

The noon mail is due now and I should get the “Post” today and a letter or so, I hope. I’ll try and write more later.

Love,

Donnie

P.S. say hello to Clarince and Ellen and the boys for me
