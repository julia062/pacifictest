August 19, 1943

Great Lakes, IL

Dear Sis,

I’ve got a little time now so I think I’ll write a page or so. I’ve got an hour than I have to go out on a work detail.

I plan on going to show tonight but I may not. I haven’t seen a moving picture since I got here. They have shows three times a week but I’ve seen them all before.

75 men in our company go on K.P. duty tomorrow for a week. My name isn’t on the list and I consider myself pretty lucky. But then I guess they will find plenty of work for the rest of us too.

I don’t remember whether I told you or not that I took out $5000.00 government insurance last week. I named you my beneficiary, so you can consider yourself lucky.

Sunday night we were raising cain and making a lot of noise in the barracks after taps. They came in and made us put on all our clothes and go out and march. I got in bed again about 10:30 and had to get up at 12:00 and go on guard duty until 4:00. I was pretty tired the next day.

Will Arnold get a 14 day furlough after he takes his exam for the army? There is a rumor here now that we may graduate the 5th instead of the 7th and if he does, I may get to see him before he leaves.

I haven’t gotten any mail for 2 days now, but I may get some in tonight's mail that comes in at 4:00 p.m. or “8 bells” navy lingo.

I’ve met 2 fellows from Blue Earth up here. One of them used to work for the rendering company. His name is Hianes. He was out to Brandsoys last spring with a load of feed. The other one’s name is Sailor. I never knew him very good. I’m going down and see Allen Hanson tonight if I get time. He leaves here the last of the week. Well I’ll have to work now, so I’ll have to quit.

Love,

Donnie
