August 7, 1942

Great Lakes, IL

Dear Sis,

I got your card today and it sure was good to hear from home. I also got a card from May.

I’m getting to like this navy life more and more every day. We have to clean the barracks every day. Tomorrow is inspection day so we really had to work today. The decks had to be waxed, all the windows had to be washed, and there couldn’t be a speck of dust left any place. By the way, how do you get clothes white? I can’t do it. I scrubbed tonight for about a half hour and they still didn’t look right. But then, I guess I’ll learn. I’ll have to.

This afternoon they marched us over to Camp Barry for a lecture. It’s about a mile over there, but I didn’t mind it. It was a whole Battalion and there were drums and bugles. There are about a thousand men in a battalion. By the way, I’m in the 1st Reg. and 1st. Bat., but it isn’t necessary to put that in my address.

I guess I told you that I sleep in a hammock. I fell out the night before last, they sure are tricky. We have to roll all of our clothes and tie them just right. They don’t take up much space when they are rolled right. It isn’t hard when you get used to it.

There is a large canteen not so far from our barracks. They have everything a sailor needs in there. There are all girls working there, but we aren’t allowed to flirt with them. They also have a studio down there. I plan to have my picture taken in a couple of weeks when my hair is grown out a little.

I have had two shots now and I will get a couple more the first of next week. We’ll be in detention for 2 ½ weeks yet. I plan to go down to Chicago on my first liberty. I may go to Milwaukee though. It’s about a half hour longer by train, but they say that one can have a lot of fun up there. So far as I know now, I’ll graduate on the 7th of September. I’ll get a nine day leave then, if something doesn’t happen. We’ll get some tests next week for trade schools.

There is a show down here tonight, but I didn’t go. Last night there was a boxing meet. I didn’t go to that either. I plan to go to the next one though. I think I’ll go out for boat racing. The winning company in a battalion gets a 36 hour liberty. We have a lot of big men in this company, so we’ll have a good chance of winning it.

I have a cold now but it isn’t so bad. I think it set in from my vaccination. It’s kind of funny weather here. When you go to bed at night, it’s hot, and in the morning, it’s colder than dickens.

I wrote to Melvin and Harold last night, and I think I’ll write to Harry and May tonight.

These fellows around here sure can make a lot of noise. The favorite subject around here is women and liquor. The only women one sees here are those in the canteen and there positively isn’t any liquor. I’ll be a good boy when I get out of here.

We have a fellow in our company. He is 17 and the youngest man here. He comes up to my waist and weighs about 100 pounds. He is the Co. pet. He can’t do anything right. We all have to help him and in the morning we set him up on the table and put his boots on for him. He is quite a guy.

Well that's about all the time I have time for, so I think I’ll quit and take a shower.

Love,

Donnie
