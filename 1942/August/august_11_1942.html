August 11, 1942

Great Lakes, IL

Dear Sis,

Boy, have I had a big day today. This morning they took us out on the drill field and we went through every exercise there is in the world, I believe. First we ran a half mile, just as fast as we could run and then we had to climb a 8 ft. wall a few times, then a tug-a-war between companies, and after that they took twenty men out of each company and had a race to see who could go over the fastest. Our company won all of it. They call it physical hardening, and they aren’t kidding just a little either. This afternoon I went to the dentist. I had two filled. Some of the fellows had teeth pulled. There are some sick boys around here tonight. Those dentists are plenty rough. They don’t care if they hurt you a little.

I’ve seen two swell orchestras this week. Sunday night I seen Von Jakobe and last night I went to the Happy Hour program. It was super and more than thatI sure had a big wash tonight. It took me about an hour. These white uniforms sure get dirty fast.

I got a letter from Harry today. He likes it okay I guess. He said his company had won out in all the barracks and personal inspections so far in the battalion. That’s more than I can say for my company. We’ve won all the athletics but we haven’t won an inspection yet. We’re poor janitors, I guess.

I went down to the canteen tonight after chow. I bought a leather toilet kit that will fit in my “dirty bag,” that was 85 cents, two pack of cigarettes 25 cents, and 10 cent ice cream bar. I gave them $5.00 and got $4.80 change. Pretty good, eh?

Yesterday, I took some service schools tests. We took three of them, Math English, and Spelling. I think we’ll get some more next week. If I can make Machinist school it would be all right. Otherwise I don’t care if I don’t make it. If nothing happens, I’ll get a 9 day leave whether I make trade school or not.

I was going to send you a paper that we get here once a week but there aren’t any left. I’ll send you one next week if I get one. I sent May and Carmon each one.

There are 14 guys writing on this table and there isn’t room to breathe hardly. It’s the noisiest bunch I’ve ever heard.

Are my clothes home yet? My receipt was lost but if they get there it's okay.

I got a service kit from the Luther League today. I guess I’ll have to write to May but I can’t think of anything to write.

Lots of love,

Donnie
